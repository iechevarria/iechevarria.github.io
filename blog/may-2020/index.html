Brick by Brick by David Robertson and Bill Breen

Brick by Brick is a little disjointed. Robertson initially set out to write a book about "rules of innovation" with several case studies, but he became fixated on the story of Lego's rise and fall. This is completely understandable - Lego's crisis years in the late 1990s and early 2000s are fascinating.

Q&A with Robert Adams and Gregory Crewdson (video)

Robert Adams is a photographer and writer. I’ve read and reread his essays more than I have any other author’s work, and I’ve given several copies of his book Beauty in Photography to friends. Adams is careful and concise in his writing, and he’s just as strong in this interview.

Some selected moments:

  • One of his least favorite parts of art is explaining his work: “Trying to explain what you’ve done has the unpleasant, embarrassing implication that you haven’t done it effectively.”
  • Adams's argument for physical prints is convincing and new to me. He says a print gives you “the opportunity to see [the image] on the wall at times when you haven’t asked to [see] it ... It helps you to know the picture better.”

I think the entire interview is worth watching, and I'd particularly recommend it if you have any interest photography.
