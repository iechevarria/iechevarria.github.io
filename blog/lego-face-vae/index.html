The high-level, handy-wavy explanation of how a VAE works

An autoencoder (AE) has two main parts: an encoder and a decoder. The encoder takes high-dimensional input (e.g., an image) and compresses it into a low-dimenstional vector representation called which I'll refer to as a latent vector. Meanwhile, the decoder takes a latent vector as input and expands it back to form of the original input.

For training, the encoder and decoder are connected, and their weights are optimized to minimize the difference between each piece of training data that enters the encoder and each piece of reconstructed data that exits the decoder.
